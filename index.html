About Research Projects

I am a licensed architect in Massachusetts and a PhD student at Stanford University in Civil and Environmental Engineering, focused on connecting building design with data-driven Life Cycle Assessment (LCA).

I worked for eight years in the architecture industry, specializing in complex facades and building systems for high-rise buildings. This hands-on experience inspired me to tackle the industry's critical challenge of high embodied carbon.

At Stanford University, I develop quantitative methods for embodied carbon reduction of building design through LCA using Large Language Models (LLMs). This research aims to create an LLM-integrated framework, such as an AI agent, that empowers designers and engineers to reduce embodied carbon, particularly during the critical early building design phases.
